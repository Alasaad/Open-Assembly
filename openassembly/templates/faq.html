What is Open Assembly?

OpenAssembly is a non-partisan site that looks to revolutionize the collaborative power of the internet. OA allows users to brainstorm a democratic platform, argue the ideal course of action, and reach consensus on those actions.

  • Discuss and Debate: OpenAssembly features a forum where you can discuss issues that are of interest to you and deliberate over the pros and cons of those issues
  • Vote: Express yourself by declaring both your subjective agreement on an idea and your opinion on the objective quality. No longer must you upvote a poorly written idea that you agree with.
  • Verify Yourself: Open Assembly features an innovative verification technique. Your vote only counts if you get out there and become active in the community. Sockpuppets, your days are numbered.

On the Horizon.

  • Polling:

    A polling system will allow the people on the ground to quickly create polls and react to events occurring around them in a decentralized manner. Small working groups on the ground and on the internet can focus their effort and make decisions quickly.

    • A user initiates a poll by inputing an email list or sending a Polling request to their followers.
    • Activists that receive the requests can browse existing choices or add their own.
    • After a time limit, or all activists have responded with choices or no addition, a one-time voting token is sent to activists.
    • Activists can vote by ranking the choices according to preference.

  • Mobile: We are working hard to adapt Open Assembly to better work on mobile devices.
  • Virtual Representative: Our adaptive platform learns your preferences to improve your experience. Each user is assigned a personalized representative that finds content you would be interested in, helps you find interesting people to connect with, and allows for detailed analysis of the opinions in the community.
  • Organize: Join coalitions of citizens that focus on issues you are most passionate about. Attend events or actions with other users to influence the real world. Photos and reputation reports will confirm your activism and persuade others to trust you. Only through trust can we ensure cooperation!

Our Philosophy

The developers of Open Assembly are dedicated to the ideals of democracy and self-governance. If a government no longer operates for the People, and by the People, that government must be removed. To truly seek a new model of democratic discourse and governance we must explore new models of democracy within our communities online and in the real world. Together we can non-violently overthrow the suboptimal democracies of the world and liberate all of mankind through a truly global human Republic.

We would appreciate support from developers, web designers or graphic designers. OpenAssembly is located on openassemblycode and licensed under New BSD. The site runs on Django-nonrel.

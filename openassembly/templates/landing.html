Open Source Democracy

Open Assembly is an Online Consensus Engine that allows groups to make decisions over the internet.

  • Temperature Check

    Find out the opinion of the group without making a formal decision

  • Timed Decision

    Make a call for proposals, actions, or policies, set the decision time and make formal decisions for the group

  • Open and Invitation Only Groups

    Open Groups to foster open discussion and closed groups to enact formal decisions. Facilitators can invite new users to the group after verifying identity with the group's own method

  • Customizable Consensus and Quorum Requirements

    Your group can set the required percentage of consent as well as what percentage of members must vote for a measure to pass

  • Schulze Method Ranked Vote to Focus the Group

    Schulze is a powerful ranked voting method. OA currently features single and multi winner ranked voting combined

  • Multi-dimensional Voting

    Citizens can vote on both their opinion and the objective quality of an argument, question or proposal

The Occupy Wall Street and Occupy Together Movement needs a General Assembly for the internet. Open Assembly is a website designed to help decentralized groups make decisions. Through innovative user verification mechanisms we will ensure that only those committed to real world activism have a voice. Together we can create policy ideas, vote on the best, and author a collaborative manifesto.

Online Working Groups

Learn More

Online working groups give you the ability to form an Open Assembly in your area with activists verified through referral. This allows you to quickly form a Group, and can create subgroups as internal small working groups. You can create polls for the group and focus the discussion with a platform election.

Build a Platform and Rally Together

Learn More

The time has come to form a coherent message of well crafted and deliberated ideas. To identify the root of our problems and the corresponding solutions we need a crowdsourced platform using the most advanced voting methods available. Open Assembly achieves this with a platform election. This helps focus the group towards unifying specific goals the group can rally behind.

If we are going to develop an online platform, we have to link the real world to the digital world. With "Anonymous Random Photo Verification" we can assure that users are real people willing to be active in the real world.
